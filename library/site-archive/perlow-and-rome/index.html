ReBar is situated just a couple of blocks away from Brooklyn Bridge Park, which boasts breathtaking views of the Brooklyn and Manhattan Bridges, and the Manhattan skyline.

NOTE: May in New York can me chilly or warm - you never know! Please dress however you feel comfortable. Our general rule of thumb is: no jeans, no tuxes, but anything in between. Most of the streets in DUMBO are cobblestone. We advise wearing or bringing shoes that are easy to walk in to avoid a wipe out!

From the BQE (I-278) Eastbound

  1. Exit on right at Cadman Plaza West
  2. Take a right at Old Fulton St. (end of ramp)
  3. Bear left at Prospect St. (1st light)
  4. Take a left at Washington St. (1st light)
  5. Take a right at Front St. (second stop sign)
  6. Proceed to 147 Front St., Between Pearl and Jay.

From the BQE (I-278) Westbound

  1. Exit on right at 28B (Brooklyn Bridge Exit)
  2. Take a right at Old Fulton St. (end of ramp)
  3. Take immediate right onto Front St.
  4. Proceed to 147 Front St., Between Pearl and Jay.

From the Brooklyn Bridge

  1. Exit right before the end of bridge for BQE/278/Cadman Plaza Keep right and bear right onto Cadman Plaza West – Keep bearing right onto Prospect St.
  2. Take a left at Washington St. (1st light) Take a right at Front St. (second stop sign)
  3. Proceed to 147 Front St., Between Pearl and Jay.

From the Manhattan Bridge

  1. Exit right before end of bridge for BQE/278/Jay Street Merge onto Jay St. Keep in left lane Take left at light on Sands St.
  2. Keep in right lane (do not board Brooklyn Bridge)
  3. Follow Sands St. around to the right. It becomes Adams St.
  4. Take a right at Front St.
  5. Proceed to 147 Front St., Between Pearl and Jay.

Here you will find places to eat, visit, and stay while you are in New York. Although we are very very partial to Brooklyn, we encourage you to explore all the boroughs; they each have their own personalities and charms. <read more>

If you want to be as close to the party as possible, we have blocked off rooms at the 2 hotels nearest to ReBar. Both are in downtown Brooklyn, walking distance to lots of great food and shopping, and only 1 subway stop (or a cheap cab ride) from the venue.

New York Marriott at the Brooklyn Bridge - $215/night - read the reviews

Nu Hotel - $209/night - read the reviews

Please reserve your rooms at least one month prior to the wedding, and mention our names, or the rates at both places will go through the roof.

We have also listed lots of hotels below in both Manhattan and Brooklyn based on things we've read (i.e. our friends' wedding website):

Manhattan:

  • The Hotel Wolcott, on 31st Street.
    This 180-room hotel has a polished-up Louis XVI-style lobby, and the rooms are tasteful; it’s popular with the young and young-at-heart because of its non-attitudy service and reasonable (for NYC) prices.
  • Herald Square Hotel, also on 31st Street.
    Rated the best budget hotel in NYC for the past 7 years, this hotel has a snazzy exterior and delightful interior.
  • Hotel 31, also on 31st Street.
    Recently renovated, with cozy (small) but tidy and bedbug-free rooms.
  • The Blue Moon Hotel, on Orchard Street.  A dramatically transformed tenement, located in the heart of the old immigrant Lower East Side directly across the street from the Tenement Museum
  • Or, try one of these New Boutique Hotels in Manhattan; this promising list was published in a recent New York Times article, and might be worth checking out.

Renting Apartments/Brownstones:

Book a private room or full apartment in a Brooklyn residence.  You can browse the available listings, read extensive reviews of apartments, see photographs, and contact potential hosts at: vrbo.com or http://www.airbnb.com/ or www.homeaway.com.  For your commuting convenience, we recommend searching for a location in these Brooklyn neighborhoods: Park Slope, South Slope, Brooklyn Heights, Carroll Gardens, Cobble Hill, DUMBO.  If you have questions about your choices, email us directly at perlowandrome@gmail.com and we’ll be glad to help you figure things out.

Just as a point of reference, we live in Park Slope.



Some reasons to visit the Slope, aside from spending some time with us in our apartment and in our garden, are:

  • Prospect Park - our sanctuary from city life. Walk/bike/run, play some frisbee, or have a picnic.
  • The Brooklyn Museum
  • The Brooklyn Botanic Garden
  • Shopping and restaurants on 5th Avenue and 7th Avenue
  • The Greenwood Cemetery - it might sound morbid but this is one of the most beautiful and tranquil places in NYC.
